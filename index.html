üîê Two-Factor Authentication Setup

Like needing two keys for your front door - your password PLUS your phone

1

Install Google Authenticator

Download from Google Play Store

  • Open Google Play Store
  • Search "Google Authenticator"
  • Install the official Google app
  • Open the app and grant camera permissions
2

Enable 2FA on Your Accounts

Add two-factor authentication to important accounts

  • Go to account security settings
  • Look for "Two-Factor Authentication" or "2FA"
  • Choose "Authenticator app" option
  • Scan QR code with Google Authenticator
  • Enter the 6-digit code to verify
3

Save Backup Codes

Keep recovery codes in a safe place

  • Download or write down backup codes
  • Store codes in a secure location
  • Don't take photos of codes
  • Each code can only be used once
1

Use Built-in Apple 2FA

Apple devices have built-in two-factor authentication

  • Go to Settings ‚Üí [Your Name]
  • Tap "Sign-In & Security"
  • Tap "Two-Factor Authentication"
  • Follow the setup instructions
2

Install Additional Authenticator

For non-Apple accounts, use an authenticator app

  • Download "Google Authenticator" from App Store
  • Or use "Microsoft Authenticator"
  • Grant camera permissions
  • Scan QR codes for each account
3

Backup Your Codes

Save recovery information securely

  • Write down recovery codes on paper
  • Store in a secure physical location
  • Consider using iCloud Keychain for passwords
  • Enable recovery contact if available
